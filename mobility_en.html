How do I get to the City of Buenos Aires?

Buenos Aires has 3 airports: Ministro Pistarini (also known as Ezeiza), Jorge Newbery and El Palomar.

From Ezeiza and Jorge Newbery, you can take the Manuel Tienda Leon Bus. You can pay in Dollars, Euros or Reales. You can book it in here.

We also have Cabify and Uber drivers. You can pay with an internation credit card or argentine pesos. Taxis are not a good option due to high prices.

Can I change money at the airport?

You can, but we suggest you change tour money in an exchange house in the city, since the airport rates are usually higher. You can find places to exchange money at Florida Street.

Keep in mind that not all places take credit cards. It is reccomended to get some argentine pesos.

What about public transportation?

We use a card called SUBE, accepted in both subways and buses. You can get one for 25 argentine pesos (about 1.50 USD), it is rechargeable and you can get one at any subway station or in some shops around the city. Subway tickets are 11 argentine pesos (0.54 USD) and bus tickets are 9.50 argentine pesos (0.47 USD).


More info on the SUBE system: here

How do I get to the Txoko?

The Txoko is located in Av. Belgrano 1144. You can get there...:

  • By subway:
    • Subte, C line, Moreno station
    • Subte, E line, Belgrano station
  • By bus: 103, 2, 45, 70, 100, 60, 10, 17, 59, 91, 9, 39, 126

To take a bus, just stop it by lifting your hand, tell the driver where you want to go, and hold your Sube card on the reader. The driver will set it up to charge your card the right amount. Some bus lines have more than one path, so always remember to ask the driver if that bus will take you to your destination

Both Google Maps and Moovit support our public bus and subway systems. There’s also the city’s official app, you can check that one in here.

Keep an eye out for your belongings, specially on crowded buses or streets.
